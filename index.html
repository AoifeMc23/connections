ARTIST STATEMENT

Aoife McNamara is a Videographer, Photographer and Visual Artist from Cork, Ireland in the early stages of her artist career. She is a recent graduate of Creative Digital Media BA (Hons) from Munster Technological University. With a passion for working with interactive technologies, she connected her knowledge and skills from her degree to explore using these interactive technologies in a larger scale art installation.

Aoife’s latest project, connections, focuses on engaging the audience with technology and each other in a shared space. Audience members are invited to engage with the installation to explore a new way of social technology. The methodologies behind her work involves thorough experiments where she explores the limits of her skills with different softwares and devices to create an interactive experience for the audience.

PROJECT PROPOSAL SUMMARY

This project “connections”, is an interactive art installation that is generated by the audience's presence. The project aims to highlight the idea social spaces are changing and becoming places of social collection. It uses interactive technologies to collectively engage the audience in a shared art piece where the audience’s input is the key factor. Without the audience, the piece doesn’t show and the more audience members there are, the more reactive and engaging the installation becomes.

The motivation for this project came from the idea that our once communal spaces such as a cafe or the park are becoming spaces of “social collection” where “people come together but do not speak to each other.” This interactive art and technology installation aims to highlight this observation, using visual arts as a medium. It utilises the audience's interactions through their audio input and hand movements to create an immersive and thought provoking experience, which is enhanced through the presence of a larger audience.

DETAILS OF PROPOSAL

There are two elements of interactive graphics that are used in this installation. The first is the audience’s audio input. The audience’s audio levels are captured through microphones around the room and brought into the software Touchdesigner. In real time, the audio input is collected and used to create graphics that are then projected onto the floor.

The second is the audience’s physical bodies, specifically their hands. Using touchdesigner, their right hands movement is tracked and projected directly onto the wall allowing the audience to uncover meaning

The project uses Touchdesigner to create the graphics with the audience's input coming from an Xbox Kinect v2 and microphones. The interactive graphics are then projected on one wall and the floor where the audience can interact with the project and immediately see the results of their connections with each other.
